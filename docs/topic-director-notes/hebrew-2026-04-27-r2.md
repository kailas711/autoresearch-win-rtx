## 2. On-thesis assessment of Verifier-1 findings

All three sub-defects are on-thesis. Binyanim are the primary morphological discriminator for Hebrew verb semantics — the difference between Qal and Piel is not voice but meaning (e.g., שׁבר Qal "break" vs. Piel "shatter/smash"). A corpus without binyan annotation for the remaining 13.8% of verbs plus the 3 named special cases has a real but bounded gap, not a hollow foundation.

Are all 3 worth fixing before Plan #6?

- **Sub-defect 1 (227 verbs, ~13.8% residual):** Yes. These are verifiable, likely mechanical, and the investigation will either reveal a pattern fix that closes most of them or confirm they're source-absent (in which case filing them as known-gaps is the correct resolution).
- **Sub-defect 2 (הלך as noun):** Yes. הלך is one of the most frequent verbs in Genesis (go/walk/live). A corpus that tags it as noun contaminates the POS signal for a high-frequency item. Fixing the normalizer logic is important for correctness, even if הלך only affects 2 fixture entries.
- **Sub-defect 3 (עשה absent):** Conditional. עשה (do/make) is extremely high frequency and critical for Genesis narrative. If it's present in source and absent from fixture, fix is required. If it's genuinely absent from `BDB Complete.atool`, file as known-source-gap and note it in `docs/domain-knowledge-cache.md`. Either resolution is acceptable — ambiguity is not.

For Plan #6 readiness: fixing ≥85% of the 227-verb residual (sub-defect 1) plus resolving the two named misses (sub-defects 2/3) brings the BDB binyan signal to a level where corpus assembly can proceed. Residual "unfixable" entries (source absent or source encoding opaque) can be treated as null-binyan and are not a blocker for Plan #6.
