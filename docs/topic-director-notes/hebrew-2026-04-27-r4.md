## 2. On-thesis assessment of Verifier-3 findings

**Is the 3,120 grammar=None count a correct measurement?**

Yes. The Verifier-3 script used the correct field (`grammar_normalized`, not `grammar`), the correct schema (`headword_consonantal` for lookups), and the correct fixture (master `lex_halot.json` at `4bfeff3`). The distribution (None=3,120, noun=2,953, verb=298, adj=167, adv=54, prep=20, interj=15, conj=5) is internally consistent: the non-None categories sum to 3,512, the None category accounts for the remainder. The measurement is confirmed.

**Is the diagnosis "binyan extractor is fine; grammar classifier upstream is broken" correct?**

Yes. Three lines of evidence converge:

1. Zero over-extraction: no non-verb entries have binyanim. If the binyan extractor were misfiring, we would see spurious binyanim on nouns. We do not.
2. High quality on the 298 verbs that are classified: 95.6% have `>=1 binyanim`, and sampled entries (אשם, יחת, יסף) show real HALOT stem-label patterns correctly extracted. The extractor works when fed.
3. Named verbs are entirely absent from the verb pool (not mis-classified with empty binyanim, but absent as verbs). נתן exists in the fixture only as `grammar=n.m.`; ראה and עשה are absent entirely. These are among the 12 most frequent Hebrew verbs in the OT. Their absence is not a binyan-extractor defect — it is a grammar-field failure that prevented them from being routed to the binyan extractor at all.

Reading the fellwork-api code confirms the likely mechanism. In `pipeline.rs::extract_grammar` (strategy `pattern_list`), the scanner does a substring search for patterns from `halot.toml` within `fields.body`. The `halot_line` field_split strategy splits on the first colon — `{headword}: {body}`. For most HALOT entries, the grammar label (`vb.`, `n.m.`, etc.) appears very early in the body, within the first few dozen characters. However, `halot_hooks.rs` documents "Grammar label scanning is limited to first 200 chars of body." If for a subset of entries the grammar label appears after position 200 in the body string — or if the body-split produces a body field that does not contain the grammar label at all (e.g., the colon split fires on a colon inside the headword's diacritic notation rather than the true headword/body separator) — the pattern_list scanner returns `None`, and the entire downstream chain (`grammar_normalized`, `classify_entry_type`, binyan extraction) receives None. This would explain why 47% of entries silently drop grammar classification while entry health reports `ok` and binyanim in the 298 classified entries are high quality.

**Does the investigation-first scope make sense, or should we go straight to a Builder fix?**

Investigation-first is correct. The HALOT decoder is more complex than BDB (per `halot_hooks.rs` own header: "HALOT has the most complex extraction logic of the four lexicons"). The root cause could be any of: (a) `halot_line` field-split firing on wrong colon, (b) 200-char body scan limit insufficient for some entry classes, (c) Aramaic/Hebrew section boundary in the binary causing a different entry layout, (d) pattern_list patterns failing to match HALOT's compact-dot labels under some body encoding. These have different fix paths of different scope. Dispatching a fix without knowing which is correct risks wrong-direction work on a 22 MB fixture. Investigation determines tier and scope; fix brief follows in round 5.

### Section C — Decoder code trace

In fellwork-api (READ-ONLY), trace the grammar extraction code path for HALOT entries:

**Primary suspects** (read in this order):

1. `crates/fw-binaries/specs/halot.toml` — `[grammar]` section: strategy is `pattern_list`, field is `body`, patterns list is `[n.pr.gent., n.pr.loc., ..., vb., adj., ...]`. 2. `crates/fw-binaries/src/decoder/pipeline.rs::extract_grammar` (line ~332): the `pattern_list` branch does a substring search for each pattern in `target` (which is `fields.body`). If so, `fields.body` starts mid-headword, and the grammar label at the true body start is present in `fields.body`. 3. `crates/fw-binaries/src/decoder/halot_hooks.rs` — line 8: "Grammar label scanning is limited to first 200 chars of body." Locate where this 200-char limit is enforced. 5. `crates/fw-binaries/src/decoder/hooks.rs::classify_from_grammar` (line ~30): `if g.starts_with("vb") || g.starts_with("v.")`. If `grammar` was extracted correctly as `"vb."`, this fires.
